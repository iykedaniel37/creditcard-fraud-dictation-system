Card Protection: Keep your credit card physically secure. Do not share your card details, PIN, or CVV with anyone. Treat your card like cash and store it in a safe place.

Online Security: When making online purchases, ensure you are on a secure website (look for "https://" and a padlock icon in the address bar). Avoid using public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks for sensitive transactions.

Strong Passwords: Create strong, unique passwords for online accounts, including your credit card account. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and special characters.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA whenever possible. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring a verification code sent to your phone or email when logging in.

Regularly Monitor Transactions: Keep a close eye on your credit card transactions. Review your monthly statements and set up alerts for any unusual activity.

Report Lost or Stolen Cards Immediately: If your card is lost or stolen, report it to your bank or credit card issuer immediately to prevent unauthorized use.

Shred Documents: Shred or destroy credit card statements, receipts, and any documents containing sensitive information before disposing of them.

Beware of Phishing: Be cautious of emails, calls, or messages asking for your credit card details or personal information. Legitimate institutions won't ask for sensitive information this way.

Use Virtual Credit Cards: Some banks offer virtual credit cards for online purchases. These provide temporary card numbers, adding an extra layer of security.

EMV Chip Cards: Use EMV chip-enabled cards for in-person transactions, as they offer enhanced security compared to magnetic stripe cards.

Fraud Alerts: Sign up for fraud alerts with your credit card issuer. They can notify you of suspicious activity on your card.

Secure Devices and Computers: Keep your devices and computers protected with up-to-date antivirus software and security patches to prevent malware attacks.

Educate Yourself: Stay informed about the latest fraud trends and prevention techniques. Awareness is a powerful tool in avoiding scams and fraud attempts.
